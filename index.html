Comparing and ordering numbers

Comparing Numbers 🔢

When comparing numbers, we look at each digit starting from the leftmost side (biggest place value).



Let us understand how to do it step by step using some examples:
Example: 5,678,901 and 3,456,789

Look at the first digit (the millions place):

In 5,678,901, the millions digit is 5.

In 3,456,789, the millions digit is 3.

Since 5 is bigger than 3, the number 5,678,901 is the bigger number.

Example 2: 5,432,189 and 5,678,123

Since the millions digits are the same, move to the next place (the hundred thousands place.

In 5,432,189, the hundred thousands digit is 4.

In 5,678,123, the hundred thousands digit is 6.

Since 6 is bigger than 4, the number 5,678,123 is the bigger number.

Example: 4,527,890 and 4,573,456

If the millions and hundred thousands digits are the same, look at the ten thousands place:

In 4,527,890, the hundred thousands digit is 2.

In 4,573,456, the hundred thousands digit is 7.

Since 7 is bigger than 2, the number 4,573,456 is the bigger number.

Example: 5,678,909 and 5,679,809

If the millions, hundred thousands and ten thousands digits are the same, move to the thousands place:

In 5,678,909 the thousands digit is 8.

In 5,679,809 the thousands digit is 9.

Since 9 is bigger than 8, the number 5,679,809 is the bigger number.

Example: 5,678,980 and 5,678,390

If the millions, hundred thousands, ten thousands and thousands digits are the same, move to the hundreds place:

In 5,678,980 the hundreds digit is 9.

In 5,678,390 the hundreds digit is 3.

Since 9 is bigger than 3, the number 5,678,980 is the bigger number.

Example: 5,678,940 and 5,678,920

If the millions, hundred thousands, ten thousands, thousands, and hundreds digits are the same, move to the tens place:

In 5,678,940, the tens digit is 4.

In 5,678,920, the tens digit is 2.

Since 4 is bigger than 2, the number 5,678,940 is the bigger number.

Example: 5,678,945 and 5,678,943

If the millions, hundred thousands, ten thousands, thousands, hundreds, and tens digits are the same, move to the ones place:

In 5,678,945, the ones digit is 5.

In 5,678,943, the ones digit is 3.

Since 5 is bigger than 3, the number 5,678,945 is the bigger number.

Summary: 🔑

  • Always start by comparing the leftmost digits.
  • If two digits are the same, move to the next digit on the right.
  • Compare each place value (millions, hundred thousands, thousands, etc.), and the number with the larger digit is the bigger number! ✨

Comparing Numbers Using Symbols 🔢

When we compare numbers, we use symbols to show which number is greater, less, or if they are equal. These symbols help us understand the relationship between two numbers.

1. Greater Than ( > ) 🦖

The symbol > means "greater than."
It shows that one number is bigger than the other.
Think of it like the open mouth of an alligator, the alligator always opens its mouth toward the bigger number because it wants to eat the bigger number.

Example: 7 > 5

The alligator's mouth is open toward 7 because it’s bigger than 5.
This means 7 is greater than 5.

2. Less Than ( < ) 🐊

The symbol < means "less than."
It shows that one number is smaller than the other.
This time, the small end of the alligator’s mouth points to the smaller number because the alligator is looking for the bigger number to eat.

Example: 3 < 6

This means 3 is less than 6.

3. Equal To ( = ) ⚖️

  • The symbol = means "equal to." It shows that both numbers are the same.
  • Example: 5 = 5 means 5 is equal to 5.
